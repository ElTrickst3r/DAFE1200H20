AIDE

Children can't study!

More than 1.6 billion children lost access to education in early April 2020 due to the corona virus outbreak. Before the virus, there were over 258 million children out of school. This is the first time in history where an entire generation had their education interrupted. Children missing out on education has great consequences, both short term, and long term.

Save The Children fear most children will not return to school after the covid-19 crisis and they do not have good enough data to analyze why children are out of school or which children will not attend their classes again. To identify and monitor students missing out on education, we want to create a system to monitor the attendance and absence of children in developing countries.

How do we solve this?

AIDE aims to solve this issue by collecting large amounts of data on the attendance of students. This data will be digitalized and sent to our AIDE global database, where the data will be analyzed by our advanced artificial intelligence. Our AI takes multiple factors into account such as the region, health, gender, travel distance and more. This ensures we get accurate information on why students are not attending school.

What makes our solution unique is how our advanced AI can also tailor specific solutions for different regions and sending these to Save the Children. The AI will also be able to pick up on outliers. For example, if it finds reports of high attendance in a region where all factors indicate otherwise.

How do we collect data?

We aim to make it easy to collect data by introducing a simple solution for use in developing countries. Every school will give out an AIDE-form to every student which logs their attendance, health, travel distance and so on. Simultaneously, the teachers will record their studentsâ€™ attendance each day to limit falsified data.

The school administration will be held responsible to collect, digitize and send the data to the AIDE database. The AIDE-forms needs to be collected each semester and attendance needs to be updated each month.

About us

AIDE was built by a group of 15 students from the Oslo Metropolitan University. We were assigned this case by Save the Children and worked together as a team to find an advanced solution but simple to use in developing countries. Our team consists of people with experience within multiple industries and diverse backgrounds which we believe is a recipe for success. We aspire to bring education to everyone, and we believe our solution could be the way to achieve this in many, if not all, developing countries.
